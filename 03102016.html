Bell state measurements are an important basic operation used in quantum information processing. Abstractly, a Bell state measurement is the projection of a two qubit state onto the Bell states. The utility of this operation can be seen in, for example, quantum teleportation.

While the concept of a Bell state measurement is straightforward, I often forget how a Bell measurement can be implemented using a simple circuit. For this reason, I am writing this very short derivation here for quick reference.

A Bell state measurement can be performed on the two qubit state \(\vert\theta\rangle\) using the following operation: $$ (\vert00\rangle\langle00\vert+\vert01\rangle\langle01\vert+\vert10\rangle\langle10\vert+\vert11\rangle\langle11\vert)(H\otimes I)(CNOT)\vert\theta\rangle, $$ where \(H\) is a Hadamard gate, which operates as: $$ H(|1\rangle) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(|0\rangle-|1\rangle) $$ $$ H(|0\rangle) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(|0\rangle+|1\rangle). $$ The \(CNOT\) gate takes the state $$ a|00\rangle + b|01\rangle + c|10\rangle + d|11\rangle $$ into $$ a|00\rangle + b|01\rangle + c|11\rangle + d|10\rangle. $$ The projection operators are just the four possible measurement outcomes. Applying these operations we find: $$ (\vert00\rangle\langle00\vert+\vert01\rangle\langle01\vert+\vert10\rangle\langle10\vert+\vert11\rangle\langle11\vert)(H\otimes I)(CNOT)\vert\theta\rangle $$ $$ (\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\vert00\rangle(\langle00\vert+\langle10\vert)+\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\vert01\rangle(\langle01\vert+\langle11\vert)+\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\vert10\rangle(\langle00\vert-\langle10\vert)+\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\vert11\rangle(\langle01\vert-\langle11\vert))(CNOT)\vert\theta\rangle $$ $$ (\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\vert00\rangle(\langle00\vert+\langle11\vert)+\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\vert01\rangle(\langle01\vert+\langle10\vert)+\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\vert10\rangle(\langle00\vert-\langle11\vert)+\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\vert11\rangle(\langle01\vert-\langle10\vert))\vert\theta\rangle $$ $$ (\vert00\rangle\langle\Phi^{+}\vert+\vert01\rangle\langle\Psi^{+}\vert+\vert10\rangle\langle\Phi^{-}\vert+\vert11\rangle\langle\Phi^{-}\vert)\vert\theta\rangle, $$ which clearly projects \(\vert\theta\rangle\) onto the Bell states.
