An important result in quantum optics is the Hong-Ou-Mandel effect [0], where two indistinguishable photons incident from different paths on a \(50/50\) beam splitter simultaneous will always leave together in the same output mode. This can be shown easily by considering the beam splitter transformations: $$ c=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(a+b), $$ $$ d=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(a-b), $$ where the \(a, b\) and \(c, d\) terms are the annihilation operators for the input and output ports respectively. The input state we are interested in then is given by \(a^{\dagger}b^{\dagger}\vert 0,0\rangle\), where the \(\vert 0,0\rangle\) state is a vacuum in each of the input modes of the beam splitter. Solving the above beam splitter relations and plugging them into this initial state results in $$ a^{\dagger}b^{\dagger}\vert 0,0\rangle=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(c^{\dagger}+d^{\dagger})\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(c^{\dagger}-d^{\dagger})\vert 0,0\rangle=\frac{1}{2}(c^{\dagger 2}-d^{\dagger 2})\vert 0,0\rangle. $$ Therefore the photons are always created in the same output port.

Less well known is that the opposite effect, where the photons always exit in different ports, can be found if we use entangled states. Specifically if we use the singlet Bell state given by $$ \vert \psi^{-} \rangle=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(a_{H}^{\dagger}b_{V}^{\dagger}-a_{V}^{\dagger}b_{H}^{\dagger})\vert 0,0\rangle, $$ where \(H\) and \(V\) stand for horizontal and vertical polarization respectively.

Since the beamsplitter we are considering is not polarizing we can rewrite the above relations as: $$ c_{j}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(a_{j}+b_{j}), $$ $$ d_{j}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(a_{j}-b_{j}), $$ where \(j\) represents the polarization.

If we again solve these relations for the input modes and plug them into the \(\psi^{-}\) state above we find [1]: $$ \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(a_{H}^{\dagger}b_{V}^{\dagger}-a_{V}^{\dagger}b_{H}^{\dagger})\vert 0,0\rangle $$ $$ =\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}[\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(c_{H}^{\dagger}+d_{H}^{\dagger})\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(c_{V}^{\dagger}-d_{V}^{\dagger})-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(c_{V}^{\dagger}+d_{V}^{\dagger})\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(c_{H}^{\dagger}-d_{H}^{\dagger})]\vert 0,0\rangle $$ $$ =\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}[c_{V}^{\dagger}d_{H}^{\dagger}-c_{H}^{\dagger}d_{V}^{\dagger}]\vert 0,0\rangle. $$ Therefore, we see that the photons will always leave in opposite arms. It's also interesting to point out the (seemingly) different normalization constants between this result and the Hong-Ou-Mandel result above. While they appear different in this form the Hong-Ou-Mandel result will pick up a \(\sqrt{2}\) when the creation operator is applied to the same vacuum mode twice.

[0] Hong, C. K., Z. Y. Ou, and Leonard Mandel. "Measurement of subpicosecond time intervals between two photons by interference." Physical Review Letters 59.18 (1987): 2044.
[1] Braunstein, Samuel L., and A. Mann. "Measurement of the Bell operator and quantum teleportation." Physical Review A 51.3 (1995): R1727.
